A weaker yen pressures the won.

Cutting rates weakens KRW further.
Hiking rates worsens domestic slowdown.

The equilibrium becomes: hold.

This is a classic imported volatility channel.

4. Why this matters for bond repacks

A bond repack is often described as:

Buy bond
Fund via repo
Pass coupons to a note

But the real trade is:

Payoff = max(0, A ? B)

Where:

A = cash RECEIVED (coupons + principal)
B = cash PAID (repo funding + closeout + recovery gap)

In stable macro:

  • Coupons accumulate smoothly
  • Funding accumulates smoothly
  • A ? B behaves linearly

In macro stress (rates spike + FX volatility):

  • Funding cost jumps
  • Bond price gaps
  • Early default probability shifts
  • Repo haircuts widen

Now A can collapse early.
B does not collapse symmetrically.

The embedded option becomes alive.

This is not just credit risk.
It is macro convexity.

5. The hidden macro linkage

Fiscal expansion ? higher yields ? funding volatility

For repacks:

Bond yield ? ? carry improves
Repo cost ? ? funding worsens

The option component is long volatility in stress states.

Ironically, the more fiscal instability you introduce,
the more valuable the limited liability floor becomes.
