The AI Doom Loop & Why Quants Must Still Read the Code

Bloomberg ran a headline this week:

“A Stock Market Doom Loop Is Hitting Everything That Touches AI.”

Investors are trapped between two fears:

1. AI will destroy business models across industries.
2. AI spending will never pay off.

Both cannot be true at the same time.

But here’s the more interesting question for markets:

If AI is rewriting the world, why are so few people learning how it
actually works?

The Quiet Shift: From Builders to Prompt Writers

Something subtle is happening.

Young engineers are skipping fundamentals.
Finance teams are skipping modeling.
Portfolio managers are skipping code.

The new belief is:
"AI will generate it."

That is dangerous.

Because in markets, generated output without understanding the engine
is leverage without knowing the Greeks.

From Linux to LLMs

I come from a Linux background.

On Linux, you learn three things:


  • You read logs.

  • You inspect processes.

  • You debug when it breaks.

AI is no different.

If you cannot read the code,
you cannot understand the model,
and if you cannot understand the model,
you cannot price the risk.

QIS in the Age of AI

This matters deeply in Quantitative Investment Strategies.

Today:


  • AI can generate a momentum model.

  • AI can optimize weights.

  • AI can backtest in seconds.

  • AI can generate marketing slides instantly.

But can AI:


  • Explain factor decay?

  • Explain transaction cost drag in stressed liquidity?

  • Model gap risk in levered vol-control?

  • Detect implicit short convexity?

Not unless the human reading the output understands those mechanics.

AI will compress the time to prototype.

It will not eliminate the need to understand:


  • replication cost

  • turnover structure

  • rebalancing logic

  • embedded optionality

  • path dependency

A QIS index is not a prompt.
It is a machine.

And machines need engineers.

The AI Doom Loop Is Really a Capital Cycle Story

The Bloomberg piece frames this as contradiction.

But what we are seeing is something more classical:

Phase 1: Narrative expansion
Phase 2: Capex surge
Phase 3: Cash flow compression
Phase 4: Valuation reset
Phase 5: Survivors compound

The AI trade is simply moving from Phase 1 to Phase 3.

Markets are not rejecting AI.
They are repricing capital intensity.

Why Code Literacy Becomes Alpha

In the next five years, the edge will not be “using AI.”

Everyone will use AI.

The edge will be:


  • knowing when the model is wrong

  • spotting structural bias in generated backtests

  • identifying survivorship distortion

  • detecting overfit signals masked as robustness

In QIS, this means:

If you cannot read the strategy construction code,
you cannot underwrite the payoff.

If you cannot underwrite the payoff,
you are buying marketing.

The Real Risk

The biggest risk is not AI displacing companies.

The biggest risk is AI displacing thinking.

Markets always punish laziness.

They especially punish blind leverage dressed up as innovation.

AI is a tool.
Linux was a tool.
Excel was a tool.

Tools increase productivity.

They do not replace literacy.

---

AI will absolutely reshape QIS.
It will accelerate strategy generation.
It will automate research.
It will reduce marginal coding cost.

But the person who understands what the machine is doing under the hood?

That person becomes more valuable, not less.

The doom loop is not about AI.

It is about who still understands the system when volatility hits.

And volatility always hits.
