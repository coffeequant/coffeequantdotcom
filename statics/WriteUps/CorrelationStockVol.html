Parameterizing Correlation between Stock & Volatility

Any volatility model can be visibly perturbed using ATMVol, Skew & Kurtosis. The model accepts these parameters and some other finer parameters to generate an extremely smooth surface.

Observations

1. When skew is very high it means OTM puts are priced at high premiums compared to ATM

2. Skew is very low OTM puts are priced at low premiums compared to ATM.

So if skew increases from low to high what is the behavior of correlation between stock and volatility?

When the market is falling drastically and volatility is increasing this implies Correlation = High. When the market is stable, and volatility is low implies that Correlation = Low.

3. Kurtosis signifies that the volatility is clustered around the mean. High kurtosis means vols are clus- tered in the middle of the distribution and index movements will not cause much change in volatility. Hence lower Correlation

4. Low Kurtosis in volatility signifies that volatility is very dispersed or that small changes in the index might cause higher volatility changes

Some quants might question this approach and say Why not just use a simple correl function to calculate correlation between index returns and volatility? Problem is stability!

Correlation Functional Form

This is similar to the function 1/(1+x). This function decreases to 0 as x approaches large values. We take the square root to make sure that value of correl is always positive. In our case x term is (kurtosis x atmvol / skew^2)

. This whole expression is scaled by 1/(sqrt(3)) for better adjustment

Based on above intuition of Kurtosis and Vols

1. Kurtosis = High implies Correlation = Low.

2. Kurtosis = Low implies Correlation = High

The value x is directly proportional to kurtosis. So when x is high we have low correlation or in other words high kurtosis = low correlation and vice versa. Similar logic applies to skew. When skew is higher it means OTM options are priced higher and might result in volatility increasing even for a slight fall in the market. So Spot movement is very much correlated with volatility when skew is high.
