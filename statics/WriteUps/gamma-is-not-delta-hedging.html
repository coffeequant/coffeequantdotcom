Gamma Is Not Delta Hedging

Gamma Is Not Delta Hedging

Most fresh traders think derivatives trading is:

“Check the delta. Take the opposite position. Done.”

If it were that simple, we wouldn’t need traders.

A derivatives trader is not a delta hedger.

A derivatives trader is a non-linear control system translating linear
models into a non-linear market.

And nowhere is this more misunderstood than gamma.

________________________________

CQX FRAME

Thesis

Long gamma and short gamma are not just risk profiles.

They are opposite liquidity behaviors.

Long gamma → you trade against the move

Short gamma → you trade with the move

Everything else is noise.

Long gamma benefits from movement.

Short gamma suffers from acceleration.

But the P&L shape is only half the story.

The execution logic is the other half.

________________________________

Long Gamma: You Are the Liquidity

If the market falls → you need to buy.

If the market rises → you need to sell.

That means:

Post bids on weakness

Post offers on strength

Let the market trade into you

You are harvesting movement and spread.

If you are long gamma and repeatedly crossing the spread aggressively?

You are leaking your edge.

Long gamma = convexity + spread capture.

________________________________

Short Gamma: You Are the Momentum

Short gamma is the opposite.

If market breaks higher → you must buy.

If market breaks lower → you must sell.

Passive quoting won’t save you.

Because the move accelerates against you.

Short gamma requires stop discipline.

Not because you’re weak.

Because gamma is acceleration.

________________________________

Trader Intuition Example

Index Vol Desk (Long Gamma)

Buys downside vol during stress.

Sells futures as market spikes.

Buys futures as market drops.

Provides liquidity during intraday swings.

The desk earns from:

Movement

Skew shifts

Spread capture

If execution is sloppy, the convexity edge disappears.

________________________________

Structured Products Desk (Short Gamma)

Short puts via autocalls.

Market gaps lower.

Delta explodes.

Forced to sell into weakness.

No stop logic → runaway losses.

Short gamma without discipline is not a strategy.

It’s delayed regret.

________________________________

The Rookie Mistake

Short gamma sitting passively on the bid or offer.

Long gamma chasing the market aggressively.

Both are misunderstandings of flow logic.

Gamma is not about being delta-neutral.

It’s about understanding:

Who provides liquidity?

Who consumes liquidity?

Who benefits from acceleration?

________________________________

The Simple Truth

Long gamma players place orders against the market.

Short gamma players use stops with the market.

Everything else is commentary.

Simple things are often misunderstood.
