Quiz Answers

Q1)The seller of an atm call option on the worst performing of 5 stocks is:

Short Volatility and Short Correlation

Explanation: If all the 5 stocks have high correlation, all of the stocks will move as one giant glob and hence high price. Let's split it into two cases

Case 1) High Correlation →

  1. All Stocks Moving in +ve Direction High Price

  2. All Stocks Moving in -ve Direction Low Price

Case 2) Low Correlation

  1. Moving in +ve Direction, chances of low price due to one stock moving back

  2. Moving in -ve Direction – LOW PRICE

So overall short correlation for seller. It's a call option so easy to see short volatility!

Q2) You go long an atm straddle and short the strangle on a vega neutral basis. Are you necessarily:

Short Skew or Short Vol Gamma or Long Delta

Explanation: Long Straddle is buy call and put at same strike and then you are selling calls and puts on different strikes. Vega Neutral means that Long Call – Short Call vega should cancel and similarly Long Put – Short Put Vega should cancel. I haven't labeled the quantities of Long Call and Short Call, but here are different cases

1. ATM options have highest vega, so straddle was at 100 Strike and strangle was purchased with higher quantity at OTM strikes.

Both of the OTM options have different impact of skew. Call and Put strikes for strangle are not mentioned so we can't predict skew behavior (surely!). Short Put OTM might have high vol / price increase effect for increased skew and Short Call OTM might have low vol / price decrease effect for increased skew.

Vol Gamma = Volga = Change in Vega due to change in vol is more predictable. But does vega change for ATM options due to change in vol! NO. How about OTM? Yes it will have

a) Vega increase for vol +ve

b) Vega decrease for vol -ve

Hence positive volga, but since we are short OTM = Short Vol Gamma, hence the answer!

Intuitive Note on Vega: If you are trading call spreads when market is low things can take a turn for worse. Positive delta position can still result in loses due to Long Vega. During the rebound of the stock you do make money on delta, but due to collapse of IV levels, you might end up losing due to +ve vega.

Vega = Positive = (V2-V1)/(sigma2-sigma1) = +ve, so V2 – V1 = -ve (since sigma2 < sigma1) or V2 < V1 or you lose money!
