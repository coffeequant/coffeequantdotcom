The trade in 20 seconds

A repack is: buy a bond, fund it in repo, and pass cashflows into a note. The ?mystery? is not the coupons. It?s the limited liability floor.

The option in one line

Define:
  • A = cash you RECEIVE from the bond (coupons + principal if no default)
  • B = cash you MUST PAY (repo funding + close-out + losses)

Payoff to the note SPV is:
max(0, A - B)

That max(0,?) is the embedded option traders price.

Toy timeline (why convexity exists)

Case 1: No default
  • A grows steadily (coupon carry)
  • B grows steadily (funding)
  • A - B stays positive

Case 2: Default in Year 2


  • A collapses early (only 2 coupons + recovery)

  • B does NOT collapse (funding already paid, unwind costs)

  • A - B becomes negative

  • But payoff floors at 0

So the downside is truncated, and that truncation is the option.
