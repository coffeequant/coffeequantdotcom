AI Isn’t Crashing the Market. Rates Are.

AI Isn’t Crashing the Market. Rates Are.

Over $1.5 trillion has been wiped from Big Tech in weeks.

Microsoft and Amazon are down more than 20% from peaks.
Nasdaq momentum has cracked.
Investors are suddenly nervous.

The headlines say:

“AI Bubble Deflating.”

But that’s not really what’s happening.




The Real Shift

For the last two years, the AI trade was simple:

  • AI boosts productivity
  • Big Tech wins
  • Earnings expand
  • Multiples rise

But now something else is happening.

Long-term interest rates are rising again.

And that changes everything.


Why Rates Matter More Than AI

Big Tech stocks are not just tech companies.

They are long-duration assets.

That means their value depends heavily on future profits — sometimes
many years out.

When interest rates rise, future profits are worth less today.

A move from 3.6% to 4.1% in the 10-year Treasury might not sound dramatic.

In valuation math, it is.

The higher the rate, the heavier the pressure on long-term growth stocks.

AI didn’t suddenly become bad.

Money just became more expensive.


Why the Selling Feels So Violent

Markets are no longer just humans reacting to news.

They are driven by:

  • Volatility-controlled funds
  • Systematic risk models
  • Options hedging flows

When stocks fall:
→ Volatility rises
→ Funds automatically reduce exposure
→ Dealers hedge by selling more

This creates a feedback loop.

That’s why declines feel sharp and mechanical.

It’s not panic.

It’s structure.


What Changed This Year

In 2023–2024, AI meant optimism.

In 2026, AI means spending.

Microsoft, Amazon, Meta and Alphabet are projected to spend more than
$600 billion this year on data centers and infrastructure.

That spending reduces free cash flow today.

Investors are asking a simple question:

“When does this pay off?”

Until that answer is clearer, rising rates make the wait harder.


So What Is the Market Pricing?

Not the death of AI.

Not the end of Big Tech.

The market is pricing:

  • Higher discount rates
  • Slower near-term returns on massive AI investments
  • Fragile positioning

If rates stabilize, this selloff can reverse quickly.

If rates continue rising, valuation pressure remains.

The driver is macro.

Not machines.


The Bigger Lesson

Every major narrative eventually collides with interest rates.

AI may transform industries.

But markets move on liquidity, discount rates and positioning.

The story is exciting.

The math is decisive.
