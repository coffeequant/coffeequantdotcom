Kelly Criteria

Kelly criteria is considered mostly in gambling but somehow has made inroads in quant finance. Somehow a more general argument which leads to a generalized formula is more efficient than Kelly. I believe kelly is just an approximation.

To keep things simple I will consider only 2 trades which makes 4 possible scenarios. There are various factors to consider when playing out those trades.

1. They might be correlated, so chances of winning the next trade might decrease based on the first outcome. (Throw kelly out of the window for that)

2. The outcomes for these two trades can have any payoff but they should add to 0 (Free Lunch DENIED)

3. N Trades = 2^N outcomes, so 2 trades = 4 outcomes. For 2 trades outcomes are Win Win or Win Lose, or Lose Win or Lose Lose.

Question I was asked on this. Where is volatility in all this?

Let me clear the confusion of volatility first. Volatility is taken out of the price, and mostly the confusion is that it's an input parameter in derivatives. mathematically yes it's true, but you do get the IV (implied volatility) from the price. And since your payoffs are taken from actual market bets, the volatility impact is already priced in! Coz once you choose the payoffs you do have to find the actual bets in the market available to generate that payoff. If vol is high the price might be high (for vega positive derivative), and vice versa. Deciding bet sizing based on volatility is gamblers approach to trading.....(i know most people will disagree with me...!).
