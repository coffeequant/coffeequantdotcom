Autocall Hedging Deep Dive

A single-stock autocall is not “short vol” as a lifestyle choice. It’s a state machine: delta/gamma/vega rotate as spot moves, time passes, skew breathes, and rates shift discounting.

1) The “Breathing” Autocall: Three Spot Regimes

Think of the autocall as a product with a probability-of-life. As spot rises, it wants to die early. As spot falls, it wants to live longer (and becomes more put-like).

Delta / Gamma Intuition (Regime Map)

The key is not “what are the Greeks today?” but “how do they migrate as spot moves?”

Vega Bucketing: When the Product “Shortens”

This is why short-dated vols can get “sticky” around popular observation calendars.

2) Numerical Example (Concrete Parameters)

One-year single-stock autocall, quarterly observation, coupon if alive, knock-in put downside. Parameters below are also what the simulator uses.

Notional = 100
Spot S0 = 100
Strike = 100, Autocall barrier = 100 (at obs dates)
Knock-in barrier = 70 (continuous monitoring simplified to daily in sim)
Coupon = 2.0% per quarter (paid if alive and above coupon barrier, simplified to alive & S≥70)
r = 4.0%, q = 1.0%, vol = 28%
Skew proxy: downside vol = vol + 8% when S below KI-zone (simple state skew)

Two “gotchas” traders learn the hard way:

  • Vanna matters near strike: spot up often means vol down (equity leverage effect). If you’re short vanna, a rally helps twice.
  • Rates matter via discounting + life extension: when uncertainty spikes, life can extend and discounting assumptions stop being cosmetic.

4) Trader Intuition Example

You’re short an autocall in the barrier zone. Spot rallies + vol softens. PnL improves from:

  • Life collapses (less time for bad things to happen)
  • Vanna (spot↑ often implies vol↓, so being short vanna helps)
  • Vega bucketing (you buy back long-dated hedges, sell front-end)

Flip it: spot drops, vol spikes. Now the product wants to live longer and become put-like. Hedging turns into short convexity management.
