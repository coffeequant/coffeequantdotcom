OutPerformance Autocallables

 

An outperformance autocallable has the payoff, Coupon on specific maturity if (A-B) > Barrier. Snowballing and other details are not important. It’s similar to a normal autocallable, with the additional risk of correlation. Correlation risk can in general be hedged according to the expected maturity of the autocallable.


For an investor correlation should be positive initially and become negative sometime before the last observation date. Positive correlation reduces the chance of autocalling, and negative correlation increases this autocall probability.


Case 1) Positive Correlation (To keep it simple let’s say 99%) - Both stocks move upward or downward

      (A-B) decreases hence low chance of autocall


Case 2) Negative Correlation (To keep it simple, let’s say -99%): Both stocks move away, so either A-B = positive or B-A = positive. If both stocks have same volatility (another simplifying assumption) then there is a 50% chance of autocalling.


So both cases demonstrate that bumping correlation is not a good idea when pricing these structures.

Consider market rise/fall scenarios as a seller of the structure.


Case 1) Markets Fall - Correlation increases!


Since we are short correlation (long dip and short coupons) there is a loss. Another thing is that probability of autocalling is less in falling market conditions.


Case 2) Markets Rise - Correlation falls


Intuitively we should have made money on this, but since the structure is ITM the correlation exposure decreases in magnitude. Hence we don’t gain as much on correlation when market increases. Of course there is 50% chance of ITM as mentioned above since if vols are equal there is equal chance of (A-B) = +ve or (B-A) = +ve. The second part (B-A) will make money.

Hedge: Apart from the usual hedges buy an “Option to Buy A COVARIANCE SWAP”. This hedges our loses incase market falls since we are short correlation. Why not correlation swap? Covariance swap hedges cross gamma effectively. In case such complicated structure “Option on COVARIANCE Swap” is not available; these have to be bought as per the current exposure and expected time to maturity. Hedges would as usual require rebalance in case of market movements.
