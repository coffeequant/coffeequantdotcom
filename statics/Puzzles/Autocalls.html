Autocallable

 

This tutorial is based on the usage of Autocallable pricer available freely on the site. There is no down and in put attached but the analysis can easily be extended with DIP attached. The structure payoff is as follows


  1. 1.Payoff a coupon 52$ (absolute) if the Index > 110 on 1 Year from Inception and wind up

  2. 2.Payoff a coupon 57$ (absolute) if the Index > 110 on 2 Year from Inception

If condition 1 is struck then structure gets Autocalled without 2nd condition being checked. Other extensions may simply add more payoffs for different maturities.


Let’s set the notation for this tutorial

Structure started at 9-Jan-2011, and in 1 year (or first fixing date) it will be 9-Jan-2012

Second fixing date similarly will be 9-Jan-2013


To understand or break apart any complicated structure try to picture the payoff’s intuitively. A crude way is


  1. 1.1 day before 1 year fixing date or on 8th January 2012 spot is at 109. If spot moves > 1 then structure is in the money, otherwise 0. Delta will be around 26 (approximately). Delta = (52-0)/(111-109) or approx 26

  2. 2.Since delta is positive value above 110 and 0 before 110, gamma will change sign. Delta around 108 - 109 level is increasing but at 110 trader needs to sell the stock to fulfill the liability. If there is time remaining the delta won’t exactly be 0 above 110, but will decrease drastically above 110. Below 110 or around 105 levels there needs to be accumulation of stock. Due to both increase and decrease of delta, we notice a sign change in gamma.

So we are Long gamma below barrier and Short gamma above the barrier

  1. 3.The same can be checked in the pricer by following fields

  1. 1.Time to maturity = 0.07

  2. 2.Coupons = 57,52

  3. 3.Coupon time in years = 0.07,0.03

  4. 4.Final Barrier = 110

  5. 5.Change graph setting to “GraphWStock” or “DeltaWStock” or “Price” for different graphs

  6. 6.Do refresh graph to change the graph only and not the price grid.

  1. 1.Introducing Time Effect


If you draw the delta / gamma graphs from above theory for 1 day expiration you will get a peaked normal distribution type shape for delta and a two humped normal distribution for gamma. So positive peaked normal for below 110 and negative peaked normal above 110 for gamma. What if time to expiry is > 1 day or, 1 year? The shape is similar but it expands around the barrier (110 in our case). Same can be checked in the pricer by increasing the time to maturity.


  1. 2.Introducing Volatility


Rather than mathematical definition, consider volatility as movement from either side from current spot. This crude version is enough for understanding the structure. Vega is defined as change in price due to change in volatility. Again let’s understand the behavior 1 day before expiry and spot is at or hovering around 109. If volatility is high, there is very high probability that stock cross the barrier or reach much below the barrier. Volatility generally has a smoothing effect on any curve. So if there is convex behavior then volatility will smoothen it out. This is also easy to understand intuitively


  1. a)If volatility is low then there is a high probability that spot will be at 111 and 108 and in between these values

  2. b)If volatility is high then there is high probability that spot will be at 105 or 115


Problem with case a) is that you will end up rebalancing the delta in positive and negative zones - remember gamma changes sign before expiry so it will cause problems. In short gamma you might end up making losses and in long gamma profits might not be that much high. In long gamma trader is hoping for big moves to make profit. So low volatility causes this gamma bleeding.


Rebalancing


A question might be asked, why choose a putspread and not a callspread. Well putspread is cheaper since calls are ITM. Still as spot/time moves the peak of vega/gamma will keep shifting. As expiry approaches basically both peaks approach the barrier. So with this shifting, it requires our hedge to be shifted. Shifting upwards the put spread is ok, coz you make gains as the high strike short put makes money. Shifting downwards will register losses. The structure price allthough decreases and should make up for the losses in hedge, but if volatility is low the hedge hedge will have greater losses. Hedge was long gamma in the lower portion. Hedge will lose theta, and gamma won’t be able to make up for it due to lower volatility. Theta is the time effect, and it is loss of option value due to passage of time. Generally known as “RENT” paid by the option owner.

Introducing Vol of Vol


Vol of vol as it says denotes volatility of volatility. If it’s high it simply means that volatility can cover a large range in a few days. If it’s low it means volatility is stable and doesn’t change much. So if volatility is high, trader will prefer a low vol of vol in long gamma range. High volatility of volatility might lead to losses in long gamma region.


Another issue is hedging, which till date I haven’t come across a hedge using vanilla’s for price changes in exotics due to vol of vol. This in-fact is the second derivative of structure price with sigma. Derivatives of the payoff which above a specific spot is positive and below that specific spot 0 (assume 1 day before expiry and spot at 109 for barrier 110), has very high sensitivity to volatility. A put spread of exactly the same expiry will replicate this behavior, but practically the put spread of same expiry might not be available in the market.


Another issue is that strikes are in steps of 10 or 20 or 100, but for replicating a specific barrier point we need very close strikes so it is just an approximation and can’t work at expiry.


One of the solutions generally used by traders is to simulate these greeks 5 - 10 days before expiry. These are priced into the structure. Another question might be why not use the heston model which automatically prices these greeks?


Problem with heston is that it assumes daily hedging and as usual the probability of the worst case happening is very low. Heston model or any other model is generating paths and averaging out the price for each of these paths. For traders an average case might not be a suitable answer to the manager - “Hey I made a loss, in one of the structures but rest assured out of 100, 60 will make money!”.
