Gamma & the rest....

2012

 

Most fresh traders think that derivatives trading is simply going long / short delta to hedge the book. Whatever the number shown in the book just take the opposite position and job over! If it was that simple we won’t need traders to do this monotonous job. “A derivatives trader by definition is a Non Linear Control hired by the firm to translate the results of _LINEAR Mathematical Models_ to non linear world”


Consider a short gamma position, and u are sitting on the bid price or let’s say the offer price. This is a rookie mistake and maybe after heavy loses you might learn that it’s better to place a stop order in short gamma position. Why? Coz you don’t want to end up chasing the market like a dog chases it’s tail unless of course you are the dog. By similar logic if you end up paying spreads when you are long gamma - simple logic is - “Trading derivatives is not for u”


Here is the reasoning...


Long Gamma


Buy Orders - Easy to execute a buy if market goes lower...so best way is to post a bid

Sell Orders - Easy to execute if market goes higher...so best way is to post an offer


Hence earn the _SPREAD_


Short Gamma


U damn need to buy when market goes higher, so posting a bid or an offer won’t help, coz long story short u become a dog chasing a tail. So in both Buy and Sell orders you need stop orders. Stop order automatically becomes a market order once there is official printing of the price.


A good long gamma player will have orders that will be mostly against the market and won’t pay the spread unnecessarily unless .......
